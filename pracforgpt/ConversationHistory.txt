### Ingredients:
- **For the Tteokbokki:**
  - 1 lb (450g) cylindrical rice cakes (tteok)
  - 4 cups water
  - 2-3 green onions, chopped
  - 2 boiled eggs (optional)
  - Fish cakes (optional)

- **For the Sauce:**
  - 2 tablespoons gochujang (Korean red chili paste)
  - 1 tablespoon gochugaru (Korean red chili flakes) - optional for extra spice
  - 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  - 1 tablespoon sugar
  - 1 teaspoon minced garlic
  - 1 teaspoon sesame oil

### Instructions:

1. **Prepare the Rice Cakes:**
   - If using dried rice cakes, soak them in water for about 30 minutes to soften. Fresh rice cakes can be used directly.

2. **Make the Sauce:**
   - In a bowl, mix together the gochujang, gochugaru, soy sauce, sugar, minced garlic, and sesame oil. Set aside.

3. **Cook the Tteokbokki:**
   - In a large pan, bring the 4 cups of water to a boil. Add the softened rice cakes to the boiling water and cook for about 5-7 minutes, or until they become soft and chewy.
   - Add the sauce mixture to the pan and stir well to combine. Let it simmer for about 5-10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens and coats the rice cakes.

4. **Add Additional Ingredients:**
   - If using, add the fish cakes and boiled eggs during the last few minutes of cooking. Stir in the chopped green onions just before serving.

5. **Serve:**
   - Once everything is well combined and heated through, transfer the tteokbokki to a serving plate and enjoy hot!

### Tips:
- You can adjust the spiciness by adding less gochugaru or more sugar to balance it out.
- Feel free to add vegetables like cabbage or carrots for extra flavor and nutrition.

Enjoy your homemade tteokbokki!
